86. Mole Negro

Mexico's most complex sauce — dozens of chiles, spices, and dark chocolate over chicken.

Ingredients:
- 4 chicken thighs, bone-in
- 4 dried mulato chiles
- 3 dried ancho chiles
- 2 dried pasilla chiles
- 2 chipotle peppers in adobo
- 2 oz dark chocolate (70%), chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1/2 onion, charred
- 1 small tomato, charred
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds, toasted
- 1/4 cup peanuts, toasted
- 1 corn tortilla, toasted and torn
- 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1/4 tsp cloves
- 3 cups chicken broth
- 2 tbsp lard or vegetable oil
- Salt

Instructions:
1. Toast dried chiles in a dry skillet; remove seeds; soak in hot water 20 minutes; drain.
2. Blend soaked chiles with charred onion, tomato, garlic, chipotles, sesame seeds, peanuts, tortilla, and spices.
3. Fry the blended paste in lard in a pot over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring constantly.
4. Add broth gradually, stirring; simmer on low for 30 minutes until thick and deeply colored.
5. Stir in chocolate until melted; season with salt; adjust thickness with more broth.
6. Brown chicken separately; simmer in the mole for 25 minutes; serve over rice with sesame seeds.

Cook time: 2 hours
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Mexican
Tags: chicken, mole, complex, chocolate
