64. Bouillabaisse

A magnificent Provençal fish stew rich with saffron, tomatoes, and a dozen varieties of seafood.

Ingredients:
- 2 lbs mixed seafood (clams, mussels, shrimp, firm white fish)
- 4 cups fish stock
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 stalks celery, diced
- 1 fennel bulb, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 tsp saffron
- 1 cup dry white wine
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fresh thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- Orange zest strip
- Rouille (garlic-saffron mayonnaise) and toasted baguette slices, for serving

Instructions:
1. Bloom saffron in 2 tbsp warm water; set aside.
2. Heat olive oil in a large pot; sauté onion, celery, and fennel until softened; add garlic.
3. Add tomatoes, wine, fish stock, saffron water, thyme, bay leaf, and orange zest; bring to a boil.
4. Simmer 20 minutes; taste and season.
5. Add seafood in order of cooking time — clams and mussels first, then fish, then shrimp; cover and cook until all are open and cooked through.
6. Discard any unopened shells; serve in deep bowls with toasted baguette and rouille.

Cook time: 50 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: French
Tags: seafood, stew, saffron, elegant
