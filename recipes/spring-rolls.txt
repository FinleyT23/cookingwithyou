89. Vietnamese Fresh Spring Rolls

Light, translucent rice paper rolls filled with shrimp, herbs, and vegetables.

Ingredients:
- 12 rice paper wrappers
- 200g cooked shrimp, peeled and halved lengthwise
- 100g rice vermicelli, cooked and cooled
- 1 cup shredded lettuce or cabbage
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 1 cucumber, julienned
- 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
- 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
- 1/2 cup fresh Thai basil
- For peanut dipping sauce: 3 tbsp peanut butter, 2 tbsp hoisin sauce, 1 tbsp lime juice, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 clove garlic minced, warm water to thin

Instructions:
1. Prepare all fillings and lay them out; mix peanut sauce ingredients, thinning with water to a dipping consistency.
2. Fill a large bowl with warm water; submerge a rice paper wrapper for 10-15 seconds until pliable but not fully soft.
3. Lay wrapper flat; place 3 shrimp halves in a line in the lower third.
4. Layer on a small amount of vermicelli, vegetables, and fresh herbs.
5. Fold the bottom over the filling, fold in the sides, then roll tightly away from you.
6. Repeat with remaining wrappers; serve immediately with peanut dipping sauce.

Cook time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Vietnamese
Tags: fresh, shrimp, no-cook, light
