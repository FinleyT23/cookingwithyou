88. Carnitas

Fall-apart, crispy-edged slow-cooked Mexican pork — the heart of a great taco.

Ingredients:
- 3 lbs pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
- 1 orange, juiced and zested
- 1 lime, juiced
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1/2 tsp chili powder
- 1 cup chicken broth or water
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp lard or vegetable oil
- Salt

Instructions:
1. Season pork generously with salt, cumin, oregano, and chili powder.
2. Place in a Dutch oven with garlic, orange juice and zest, lime juice, broth, and bay leaves.
3. Bring to a boil; cover and cook at 325°F for 2.5 hours until very tender.
4. Uncover and increase oven to 450°F; cook 20-30 more minutes until liquid evaporates and pork crisps up on the bottom.
5. Remove bay leaves; shred pork with two forks, mixing crispy bits throughout.
6. Serve in warm corn tortillas with salsa, onion, cilantro, and lime.

Cook time: 3 hours
Servings: 6
Cuisine: Mexican
Tags: pork, slow-cook, tacos, crispy
