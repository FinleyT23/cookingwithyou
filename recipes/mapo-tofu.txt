18. Mapo Tofu

A fiery Sichuan classic with silken tofu in a numbing, spicy bean sauce.

Ingredients:
- 1 block silken or soft tofu, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 200g ground pork
- 2 tbsp doubanjiang (spicy bean paste)
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorn, toasted and ground
- 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
- Green onions, for garnish

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high; fry doubanjiang until fragrant and oil turns red.
2. Add garlic, ginger, and black beans; cook 1 minute.
3. Add ground pork and cook until browned.
4. Pour in broth, soy sauce, and sugar; bring to a simmer.
5. Gently slide in tofu; simmer 3 minutes without stirring too much.
6. Stir in cornstarch slurry to thicken; sprinkle with Sichuan pepper and green onions; serve over rice.

Cook time: 25 minutes
Servings: 3
Cuisine: Chinese
Tags: spicy, tofu, quick, numbing
