82. Beef and Broccoli

A takeout classic done right — tender beef in a glossy, garlicky oyster sauce.

Ingredients:
- 1 lb flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
- 3 cups broccoli florets
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ginger, minced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce (for marinating) + 3 tbsp for sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp cornstarch + 1 tbsp for sauce
- 1/2 cup beef broth
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- Steamed rice, for serving

Instructions:
1. Marinate beef with 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp cornstarch, and sesame oil for 20 minutes.
2. Mix sauce: 3 tbsp soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin, broth, and 1 tbsp cornstarch; set aside.
3. Blanch broccoli in boiling water for 2 minutes; drain.
4. Stir-fry beef in a hot wok with oil in batches until just browned; remove.
5. Sauté garlic and ginger in the same wok; return beef and add broccoli.
6. Pour sauce over everything; toss until sauce thickens and coats everything evenly; serve over rice.

Cook time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Chinese-American
Tags: beef, quick, stir-fry, takeout
