39. Brazilian Feijoada

Brazil's national dish — a rich black bean stew with smoky pork and sausage.

Ingredients:
- 2 cups dried black beans, soaked overnight
- 300g smoked pork ribs
- 200g chorizo or smoked sausage, sliced
- 150g bacon, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp cumin
- Salt and pepper
- White rice, collard greens, orange slices, and farofa, for serving

Instructions:
1. Drain soaked beans; cover with fresh water in a large pot; bring to a boil; reduce and simmer 30 minutes.
2. In a separate skillet, render bacon; sauté onion and garlic until soft.
3. Add sausage and pork ribs to the bacon; cook until browned; transfer everything to the bean pot.
4. Add bay leaves and cumin; simmer all together for 1-1.5 hours until beans are very tender.
5. Remove pork ribs and shred any meat from bones; return meat to pot; season with salt and pepper.
6. Serve with white rice, sautéed collard greens, orange slices, and farofa.

Cook time: 2.5 hours (plus soaking)
Servings: 6
Cuisine: Brazilian
Tags: beans, pork, stew, slow-cook
