63. Birria Tacos

Slow-braised Mexican beef tacos dipped in rich, red consommé and crisped on the griddle.

Ingredients:
- 3 lbs beef chuck or short rib
- 4 dried guajillo chiles
- 2 dried ancho chiles
- 2 chipotle peppers in adobo
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 onion, quartered
- 5 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 4 cups beef broth
- Corn tortillas, Oaxacan cheese, diced onion, cilantro, and lime, for serving

Instructions:
1. Toast dried chiles in a dry pan; soak in hot water for 20 minutes; drain.
2. Blend chiles, chipotle, tomatoes, onion, garlic, and spices with 1 cup broth until smooth.
3. Sear beef in a heavy pot; pour chile sauce and remaining broth over; cover and braise at 325°F for 3 hours until fall-apart tender.
4. Remove beef and shred; skim fat from the braising liquid (consommé) and reserve for dipping.
5. Dip a corn tortilla in the consommé; fry on a griddle with cheese and shredded beef until crispy; fold.
6. Serve with consommé for dipping, topped with onion, cilantro, and lime.

Cook time: 3.5 hours
Servings: 6
Cuisine: Mexican
Tags: beef, tacos, slow-cook, crispy
