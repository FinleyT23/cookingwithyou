69. Tonkotsu Ramen

A milky, rich pork bone broth ramen that takes time but delivers extraordinary depth.

Ingredients:
- 2 lbs pork leg bones
- 300g chashu pork belly (see steps below)
- 200g ramen noodles (fresh preferred)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp mirin
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- Soft-boiled marinated eggs (ramen eggs), 2 green onions, 2 sheets nori, corn, bamboo shoots

Instructions:
1. Blanch pork bones in boiling water for 10 minutes; scrub clean; transfer to a large pot.
2. Cover with 12 cups of water; boil vigorously uncovered for 4-6 hours, adding water as needed, until broth is creamy white and rich.
3. For chashu: roll pork belly tightly, tie with twine; sear all sides; braise in 1/4 cup each soy sauce, mirin, and water for 1.5 hours; slice.
4. Strain broth; season with soy sauce, mirin, and sesame oil; keep very hot.
5. Cook ramen noodles; divide into large bowls.
6. Ladle boiling broth over noodles; top with chashu, ramen egg, green onion, nori, corn, and bamboo shoots.

Cook time: 6 hours
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Japanese
Tags: ramen, pork, noodles, labor-of-love
