46. Boeuf Bourguignon

The definitive French beef braise — slow-cooked in red wine with mushrooms and pearl onions.

Ingredients:
- 3 lbs beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
- 1 bottle Burgundy or Pinot Noir
- 200g lardons or thick bacon, diced
- 1 cup pearl onions, peeled
- 2 cups cremini mushrooms, quartered
- 3 carrots, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp flour
- 1 cup beef broth
- 2 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves, fresh parsley
- 3 tbsp butter
- Salt and pepper

Instructions:
1. Marinate beef in wine with vegetables overnight; strain, reserving marinade and beef separately.
2. Brown lardons in a Dutch oven; brown beef in batches; remove both.
3. Cook carrots, garlic, and tomato paste; stir in flour; pour in reserved marinade and broth.
4. Return beef and lardons; add herbs; bring to a simmer.
5. Cover and braise in a 325°F oven for 2.5-3 hours until beef is very tender.
6. Sauté pearl onions and mushrooms in butter until golden; add to finished stew; discard herbs; serve.

Cook time: 3.5 hours (plus overnight marinade)
Servings: 6
Cuisine: French
Tags: beef, braise, wine, slow-cook
