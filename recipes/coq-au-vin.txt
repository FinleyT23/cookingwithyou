16. Coq au Vin

A French bistro classic of chicken slowly braised in red wine with mushrooms and lardons.

Ingredients:
- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
- 1 bottle red Burgundy wine
- 200g lardons or thick-cut bacon, diced
- 2 cups cremini mushrooms, quartered
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 2 sprigs thyme, 2 bay leaves
- 2 tbsp flour
- 2 tbsp butter
- Salt and pepper

Instructions:
1. Marinate chicken in wine with aromatics overnight; remove chicken and reserve marinade.
2. Brown lardons in a Dutch oven; remove; brown chicken in the same fat and remove.
3. Sauté onion, carrots, and garlic; stir in tomato paste and flour; cook 2 minutes.
4. Pour in reserved marinade and broth; return chicken and lardons; add herbs.
5. Cover and braise in a 325°F oven for 1.5 hours.
6. Sauté mushrooms in butter until golden; stir into finished stew; serve.

Cook time: 2 hours (plus overnight marinade)
Servings: 6
Cuisine: French
Tags: braise, chicken, wine, slow-cook
