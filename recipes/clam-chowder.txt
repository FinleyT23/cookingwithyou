23. New England Clam Chowder

A thick, creamy soup loaded with clams, tender potatoes, and smoky bacon.

Ingredients:
- 2 cans (10oz each) chopped clams, with juice
- 4 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 stalks celery, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 cups Yukon Gold potatoes, cubed
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 1 cup whole milk
- 3 tbsp butter
- 3 tbsp flour
- 1 tsp fresh thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt, white pepper, and oyster crackers, for serving

Instructions:
1. Cook bacon in a large pot until crispy; remove and set aside, leaving fat in pot.
2. Sauté onion, celery, and garlic in bacon fat until soft; add butter and flour; stir 2 minutes.
3. Drain clam juice from cans into the pot; add potatoes, thyme, and bay leaf; simmer until potatoes are tender.
4. Stir in cream and milk; bring to a gentle simmer; do not boil.
5. Add clams; cook 5 minutes just until warmed through.
6. Season, remove bay leaf, and serve topped with bacon and oyster crackers.

Cook time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: American
Tags: soup, seafood, creamy, New England
