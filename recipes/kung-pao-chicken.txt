54. Kung Pao Chicken

A Sichuan stir-fry with tender chicken, toasted peanuts, and a punchy sauce.

Ingredients:
- 1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, diced
- 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
- 8-10 dried red chiles
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced
- 3 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- For sauce: 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp cornstarch, 4 tbsp water
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorn, toasted and ground

Instructions:
1. Mix sauce ingredients together and set aside; toss chicken with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp cornstarch.
2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat; stir-fry dried chiles until dark and fragrant; remove and set aside.
3. Stir-fry chicken in batches until golden; remove.
4. Add garlic and ginger to the wok; stir-fry 30 seconds; return chicken and chiles.
5. Pour sauce over everything and toss to coat; cook until sauce thickens.
6. Toss in peanuts and green onions; sprinkle with Sichuan pepper; serve over rice.

Cook time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Chinese
Tags: chicken, stir-fry, spicy, quick
