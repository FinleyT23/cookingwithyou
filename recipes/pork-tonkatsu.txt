92. Pork Tonkatsu

Japanese panko-fried pork cutlet — golden, crispy, and served with tangy Worcestershire-based sauce.

Ingredients:
- 4 pork loin chops (about 3/4 inch thick), trimmed
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/3 cup flour
- 2 eggs, beaten
- Salt and black pepper
- Oil for deep-frying
- Tonkatsu sauce: 3 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 2 tbsp ketchup, 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sugar
- Shredded cabbage, steamed rice, and Japanese mustard, for serving

Instructions:
1. Pound pork chops to 1/2 inch thickness; season with salt and pepper.
2. Dredge each chop in flour, shaking off excess; dip in beaten egg; coat generously in panko.
3. Press panko firmly onto the pork; rest on a rack for 10 minutes so coating adheres.
4. Fry in 350°F oil for 5-6 minutes, turning once, until golden brown and cooked through (165°F internal).
5. Drain on a rack; mix tonkatsu sauce ingredients.
6. Slice pork on an angle; serve over shredded cabbage and rice with sauce drizzled over.

Cook time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Japanese
Tags: pork, fried, crispy, comfort
