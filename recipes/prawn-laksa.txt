87. Prawn Laksa

A rich, coconut-based Malaysian noodle soup with depth from a homemade spice paste.

Ingredients:
- 400g large prawns, shell-on
- 200g rice vermicelli noodles, soaked
- 1 can (14oz) coconut milk
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 100g bean sprouts
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- For laksa paste: 4 shallots, 3 cloves garlic, 1-inch galangal, 1-inch ginger, 3 dried chiles rehydrated, 1 stalk lemongrass (white part), 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tbsp shrimp paste, 1 tbsp coriander
- Hard-boiled eggs, tofu puffs, lime, and fresh mint or Vietnamese coriander, for serving

Instructions:
1. Blend all laksa paste ingredients into a smooth paste, adding a splash of water if needed.
2. Fry paste in a wok with oil over medium heat for 8-10 minutes, stirring, until very fragrant and oil separates.
3. Add coconut milk and broth; bring to a gentle simmer; taste for seasoning.
4. Add prawns and simmer 3-4 minutes until cooked through.
5. Divide soaked noodles and bean sprouts into bowls; ladle hot laksa broth and prawns over.
6. Top with halved eggs, tofu puffs, and fresh herbs; serve with lime.

Cook time: 30 minutes
Servings: 2
Cuisine: Malaysian
Tags: noodles, seafood, coconut, spicy
