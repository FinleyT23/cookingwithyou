85. Bibimbap

A Korean rice bowl piled with colorful seasoned vegetables, beef, and a fried egg, unified with gochujang.

Ingredients:
- 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
- 200g ground beef or sliced beef
- 1 tbsp soy sauce + 1 tsp sesame oil (for beef)
- 1 cup spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
- 1 carrot, julienned and sautéed
- 1 zucchini, julienned and sautéed
- 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched
- 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms, sautéed
- 2 eggs, fried sunny-side up
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- 1 tbsp sesame oil + 1 tbsp for sauce
- 1 tsp sugar + toasted sesame seeds

Instructions:
1. Cook beef with soy sauce and sesame oil until browned; set aside.
2. Season each vegetable component separately with a pinch of salt and a few drops of sesame oil.
3. Mix gochujang, sesame oil, and sugar into a sauce; thin with a splash of water.
4. Divide warm rice into two bowls; arrange vegetables, beef, and egg artfully on top in sections.
5. Drizzle gochujang sauce over everything; sprinkle with sesame seeds.
6. Serve immediately; mix everything together vigorously before eating.

Cook time: 40 minutes
Servings: 2
Cuisine: Korean
Tags: rice-bowl, beef, vegetables, spicy
