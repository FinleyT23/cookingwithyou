65. Doro Wat with Injera

Ethiopia's most celebrated dish — slow-simmered spiced chicken stew served on spongy flatbread.

Ingredients:
- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
- 4 large onions, very finely diced
- 4 tbsp berbere spice blend
- 4 tbsp niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) or regular butter
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp ginger, grated
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and pricked with a fork
- Salt
- Store-bought injera, for serving

Instructions:
1. Cook onions in a dry pot over medium-low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring often, until very soft and reduced to a thick paste — no oil yet.
2. Add niter kibbeh and berbere; fry together for 10 minutes until the fat turns deep red.
3. Add garlic, ginger, and broth; stir in chicken pieces; cover and simmer for 45 minutes.
4. Add hard-boiled eggs and simmer uncovered 15 more minutes until sauce thickens and chicken is very tender.
5. Season with salt.
6. Serve over injera with eggs on top; tear injera to scoop and eat.

Cook time: 1.5 hours
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Ethiopian
Tags: chicken, stew, spiced, slow-cook
