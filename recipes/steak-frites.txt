90. Steak Frites

The iconic French bistro pairing — a perfectly seared steak with crispy golden fries.

Ingredients:
- 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks (about 10oz each)
- 4 large Russet potatoes, cut into thin fries
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 cloves garlic, smashed
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- Oil for deep-frying
- Salt and black pepper
- For pan sauce: 1/4 cup red wine, 1/2 cup beef broth, 1 tbsp butter, 1 tsp Dijon

Instructions:
1. Double-fry fries: first at 325°F for 5 minutes; drain; then at 375°F for 3-4 minutes until crispy; season immediately with salt.
2. Pat steaks dry; season generously with salt and pepper at least 30 minutes before cooking.
3. Sear steaks in a very hot cast iron skillet with a little oil, 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare.
4. Add butter, garlic, and thyme; baste steaks continuously for 1 minute; rest on a board.
5. Deglaze pan with wine; add broth; simmer until reduced by half; whisk in butter and Dijon.
6. Slice steaks; serve with fries and pan sauce alongside.

Cook time: 45 minutes
Servings: 2
Cuisine: French
