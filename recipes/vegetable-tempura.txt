97. Vegetable Tempura

Impossibly light and crispy Japanese battered vegetables served with a delicate dipping broth.

Ingredients:
- 1 sweet potato, sliced 1/4 inch thick
- 1 zucchini, sliced
- 1 bell pepper, cut into strips
- 8 green beans
- 8 shiitake mushrooms
- For batter: 1 cup cold flour, 1 egg yolk, 1 cup very cold sparkling water
- Oil for deep-frying
- For tentsuyu dipping sauce: 1 cup dashi or chicken broth, 3 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp mirin, grated daikon and ginger

Instructions:
1. Make tentsuyu: heat dashi, soy sauce, and mirin together; keep warm; serve with grated daikon alongside.
2. Chill all vegetables thoroughly — cold ingredients help the batter stay light.
3. Mix batter just before frying: whisk egg yolk and sparkling water together; add flour all at once; stir briefly — lumps are fine; do not overmix.
4. Heat oil to 340°F; dip cold vegetables in batter; let excess drip; fry a few pieces at a time.
5. Fry 2-3 minutes until batter is just barely golden and very lacy; drain on a rack.
6. Serve immediately with dipping sauce and grated daikon.

Cook time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Cuisine: Japanese
Tags: vegetarian, fried, light, crispy
